The source report lives outside the repo (`/private/tmp/...`) and was supplied by the maintainer; archived here as the re-verification record, not as a repo-resident audit.

## Verdict

A **competent breadth audit** — its praise of the security primitives (constant-time auth, realpath guards, OCR-offline enforcement), coverage discipline, and SLSA L2 all spot-check **true**. But it has **one material miss** and several **factual errors**, so its "production-ready, no critical findings" headline must not be taken at face value, and it does **not** alone satisfy the v3.6.1 "≥2 independent external auditors" gate for `@latest`.

## The material miss

The report graded `d564eb5` (rc.24) as **"Critical Priority Findings: None."** That commit contained a genuine **CRITICAL**: a remotely-triggerable ReDoS in `obsidian_open_questions` (`(a?b|b)+$` pegs V8 for seconds on bearer-auth `serve-http`; the tool is always-registered). Verified: rc.24 had **zero** of the guard helpers (`leadingAtomSet`/`branchIsNullable`/`bodyHasVariableQuantifier`) — it was vulnerable. Our **internal** audit found it and we fixed it in **rc.25** (+ a permanent generative fuzz harness). The Mavis security track missed it.

## Lesson

Reinforces the v3.6.1 rule: a single external auditor — even a competent one — can miss the one finding that matters most (here, a live remote-DoS CRITICAL that our own state-driven + adversarial-fuzz methodology caught). The `@latest` gate must still want a **second independent external pass with a different methodology**.
